Rocket Flight Dynamics & Guidance Simulation

A full-flight, 6-DOF-inspired simulation environment for a reusable booster: from liftoff, to downrange boostback, to a powered landing â€” wired into FlightGear for real-time visualization.

Impact

  • Cut trajectory tuning from hours of manual scripting to minutes using reusable parameter sets and mission profiles.
  • Landing guidance consistently returns within <60 miles of the target in simulated booster recoveries.
  • Provides a reusable sandbox for trying out new guidance laws without touching hardware.

Problem

Most student rocket projects stop at apogee. I wanted to understand what it takes to fly a reusable booster end-to-end: ascent, flip, boostback, entry, and landing, and how guidance decisions show up in real trajectories.

Approach

    Simulation architecture:
  • Implemented planar rocket equations of motion with gravity, drag, thrust, and time-varying mass.
  • Built an ISA-based atmosphere model to compute density and drag through troposphere and lower stratosphere layers.
  • Structured the Simulink model as modular blocks (atmosphere, dynamics, guidance, control) so components can be swapped independently.
    Guidance & control:
  • Programmed a gravity turn and phase-based guidance logic that sequences ascent, flip, boostback, entry burn, and landing burn.
  • Implemented a first-order pitch control loop so the vehicle tracks commanded pitch smoothly without overshoot.
  • Tuned guidance parameters to respect thrust limits, dynamic pressure envelopes, and landing constraints.
    Visualization & tooling:
  • Streamed state vectors to FlightGear via UDP at real-time rates for 3D visualization.
  • Added logging for altitude, velocity, attitude, and engine events to quickly compare guidance variations.
