Rocket Flight Dynamics & Guidance Simulation
Solo MATLAB & Simulink ¬∑ Simulation

Objective: Model a reusable rocket booster from launch to landing and see how guidance and aerodynamics change the full flight path.

My role: Designed and implemented the full simulation stack, from equations of motion and atmosphere modeling to guidance logic and visualization.

    Process:
  • Implemented 2D rocket equations of motion in MATLAB and Simulink, including gravity, drag, thrust, and fuel mass depletion.
  • Built an atmosphere model using ISA lapse rates to compute density and drag across troposphere and stratosphere layers.
  • Programmed a gravity turn and pitch command logic that handles ascent, flip, boostback, entry, and landing phases.
  • Designed a first-order pitch control loop so the rocket smoothly tracks commanded pitch without overshoot.
  • Streamed state data to FlightGear over UDP for real-time 3D visualization of the full launch and landing profile.
    Outcome:
  • Produced a reusable MATLAB/Simulink tool that can simulate Falcon-9-style booster missions with tunable vehicle and guidance parameters.
  • Used the sim to visualize how changes in thrust profile, drag, or guidance laws affect apogee, downrange distance, and landing conditions.
