Thrust Vector Control (TVC) Prototype
Team project · Mechatronics

Objective: Build a small-scale thrust-vectoring gimbal to simulate rocket steering and study dynamic stability.

My role: Co-led design, mechanical layout, and control logic implementation for the two-axis gimbal.

  • Process:
  • Sketched concepts and selected a two-axis gimbal architecture sized for servo torque, stiffness, and travel limits.
  • Modeled and 3D-printed the gimbal in SolidWorks, iterating clearances and mounting features for the motor and IMU.
  • Integrated an MPU6050 IMU and high-torque servos, routing wiring and defining hard-stop angles.
  • Programmed an Arduino control loop processing 100+ IMU readings per second with an auto-zero routine.
  • Outcome:
  • Achieved repeatable thrust vectoring within ~1–2° of commanded angle for lab-scale rocket simulations.
  • Created a reusable testbed for validating control algorithms before flight hardware is built.

Door Lock Actuator Rebuild
Solo repair · Automotive electronics

Objective: Restore a failing power door lock without replacing the full latch assembly by rebuilding the internal actuator.

My role: Performed diagnosis, motor selection, mechanical rebuild, and on-car verification testing.

  • Process:
  • Removed the latch/actuator assembly and opened the sealed OEM AISIN actuator housing.
  • Inspected the gear train, contacts, and motor to confirm the motor was the failure point.
  • Matched a replacement motor by researching part numbers, dimensions, shaft profile, and operating voltage.
  • Installed the new motor, re-timed the gears, cleaned internal contacts, and re-sealed the case with epoxy resin.
  • Outcome:
  • Door now locks and unlocks reliably from the key fob and interior switch.
  • Part is no longer manufactured, making repair the only viable option while preserving full OEM fitment and linkage.
  • Documented the procedure so other doors (or friends’ cars) can be repaired the same way.
Diagnostics DC motors Epoxy sealing Automotive repair
